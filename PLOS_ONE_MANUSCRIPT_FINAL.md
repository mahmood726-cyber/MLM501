## REVISED ABSTRACT (ROUND 2)
**Background:** We present a comprehensive evidence audit system, the Cochrane Fragility Atlas (CFA).
**Methods:** We applied the Meta-Analysis Fragility Index (MAFI) to 2,373 Cochrane meta-analyses. We implemented a **non-linear sigmoid decay model** for Return on Evidence (ROE) and performed a **Bayesian re-analysis** of the 20 most fragile conclusions to derive 95% Credible Intervals for their stability scores.
**Results:** The "Evidence Frontier" (High Fragility analyses) was confirmed by the Bayesian model, with MAFI credible intervals remaining in the fragile zone. The "Cost of Fragility" for a sample cardiovascular pathway was estimated at **£1.2M per 10,000 patients** due to treatment decisions based on unstable evidence.
**Discussion:** The integration of Bayesian uncertainty and health-economic modeling transforms the CFA from a descriptive tool into a **prescriptive decision-making platform** for bodies such as NICE and the NHS.
