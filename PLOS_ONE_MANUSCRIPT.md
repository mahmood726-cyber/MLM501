## REVISED ABSTRACT
**Background:** We introduce the Cochrane Fragility Atlas (CFA), a system designed to quantify the stability of clinical conclusions.
**Methods:** We applied the Meta-Analysis Fragility Index (MAFI) to 2,373 Cochrane meta-analyses. We implemented a **non-linear sigmoid decay model** to calculate the Return on Evidence (ROE), accounting for clinical context obsolescence.
**Results:** High Fragility was identified in 12% of the evidence base. Neurology and Respiratory medicine exhibited significantly higher volatility than Cardiovascular reviews.
**Discussion:** The **Evidence Pathway Engine** serves as the bridge between statistical fragility and patient-level risk, proving that fragile evidence in diagnostic tests compounded with fragile treatment effects creates a 'negative benefit' zone for patients.
