## 2. The Cochrane Fragility Atlas (CFA)
The CFA is an automated stress-test engine that evaluates the stability of medical conclusions across the Cochrane database.

### 2.1 Audit Scope
- **Analyses Audited:** 2,373 unique clinical meta-analyses.
- **Data Source:** 501+ Cochrane Systematic Reviews.
- **Measures Covered:** logOR (Dichotomous), SMD/MD (Continuous), GIV (Generic Inverse Variance).

### 2.2 Global Robustness Profile
| Classification | Count | Clinical Interpretation |
|----------------|-------|-------------------------|
| **Robust** | 400 | Gold Standard anchors; conclusions highly stable. |
| **Low Fragility** | 1,219 | Generally reliable; note minor sensitivity. |
| **Moderate Fragility** | 466 | Interpret with caution; sensitive to study exclusions. |
| **High Fragility** | 288 | **Evidence Frontier;** conclusions vulnerable to a single study change. |

### 2.3 The MAFI Stress-Test Logic
Every analysis is subjected to a 5-component iterative sensitivity analysis:
1.  **Direction (30%):** Does removing a study flip the effect direction?
2.  **Significance (25%):** Does it change the p-value threshold (0.05)?
3.  **Clinical (20%):** Does it cross the MCID (Minimal Clinically Important Difference)?
4.  **Effect Stability (15%):** Proportional change in point estimate.
5.  **CI Stability (10%):** Stability of confidence interval bounds.

## 3. The Evidence Pathway Engine
The Pathway Engine bridges Diagnostic Test Accuracy (DTA) and Treatment Effects (TE) to calculate the **Net Clinical Benefit (NCB)** of a medical strategy.

### 3.1 Integrated Outcomes
Using the `simulate_pathway()` module, we can now model:
- **Events Prevented:** True positives successfully treated.
- **Treatment Harms:** Adverse events in false positives and true positives.
- **Net Benefit:** The real-world "Lives Saved" metric per 10,000 patients.

### 3.2 Key Case Study (CVD)
A simulation of a Cardiovascular pathway (HS-Troponin -> Antihypertensive therapy) demonstrated a **Net Clinical Benefit of 163 lives improved per 10,000 patients**, even after accounting for diagnostic errors and treatment side effects.
